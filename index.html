THE HISTORY OF NUCLEAR TESTING




The Trinity Test

July 16, 1945

The Trinity test marked the beginning of testing nuclear weapons. Developed as part of the Manhattan Project, this 20kT device was detonated in the desert of New Mexico. Since this event, over 2000 nuclear tests have been run througout the world.

First Lightning

August 29, 1949

The Russian First Lightening test. This the first test conducted outside of the control of the United States Military and was in response to the American's bomb. The test signaled the end of a nuclear monopoly, and the start of an arms race.

Partial Test Ban Treaty

October 10, 1963

The Partial Test Ban Treaty banned the testing of nuclear weapons in the atmosphere, in space, and underwater. The treaty was an attempt to slow rapid pace of testing and helped reduce the amount of radioactive fallout that was a result of nuclear testing. Unfortunately, the treaty pushed the testing of nuclear devices underground.
