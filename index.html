THE HISTORY OF NUCLEAR TESTING




The Trinity Test

July 16, 1945

The Trinity test marked the beginning of testing nuclear weapons. Developed as part of the Manhattan Project, this 20kT device was detonated in the desert of New Mexico. Since this event, over 2000 nuclear tests have been run througout the world.

First Lightning

August 29, 1949

The Russian First Lightening test. This the first test conducted outside of the control of the United States Military and was in response to the American's bomb. The test signaled the end of a nuclear monopoly, and the start of an arms race.

Continual Testing

1945-1996

From 1945 to 1996, over 2000 tests were conducted worldwide. In this time, only five countries conducted test, with The United States carrying out over half of all the tests. At this rate, there was on average one test being conducted every nine days!

Partial Test Ban Treaty

October 10, 1963

The Partial Test Ban Treaty banned the testing of nuclear weapons in the atmosphere, in space, and underwater. The treaty was an attempt to slow rapid pace of testing, and helped reduce the amount of radioactive fallout that was a result of nuclear testing. Unfortunately, the treaty pushed the testing of nuclear devices underground.

Comprehensive Nuclear-Test-Ban Treaty

September 10, 1996

The Comprehensive Nuclear-Test-Ban Treaty attempts to ban all nuclear explosions. The ban on testing weapons will make it a challenge for countries without nuclear weapons to develop them, and difficult for countries with an arsenal to develop more powerful weapons.

Testing After the CTBT

1996-Present

Since the treaty was signed, there have been around 6 tests conducted. The monitoring systems put in place by the CTBTO have detected all of the tests conducted by North Korea. 183 countries have signed the treaty, but of those 19 have yet to ratify it.
